About

I am a Professor and the Idaho Wheat Commission Bill Flory Endowed Chair in Risk Management at the University of Idaho. My research focuses on risk management, price analysis and forecasting, and futures and options markets, with additional work in applied econometrics, consumer marketing, and policy analysis.

I earned my Ph.D. in Agricultural and Applied Economics from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2013, with a dissertation on bubbles in agricultural futures markets, along with an M.S. in Applied Statistics. My work has appeared in journals including Nature Communications, the American Journal of Agricultural Economics, Food Policy, World Development, Energy Economics, and the Journal of Futures Markets, and has been covered by the Financial Times and Reuters. As principal or co-principal investigator I have received over $4.4 million in funded research, including support from USDA NIFA, USDA ERS, and the National Science Foundation.

Before joining the University of Idaho in 2021, I was on the faculty at West Virginia University and worked as a consultant for the World Bank Group. I also hold adjunct appointments at Washington State University and West Virginia University.

Research

My current work falls into four broad areas:

  • Price behavior, bubbles, and forecasting. Detecting speculative bubbles and price explosiveness in grain and food markets, and building forecasts of farm and food prices that hold up during rapid economic change.
  • Futures, options, and risk management. How producers hedge, how risk premiums form in commodity futures, and which marketing tools reduce the price risk growers carry.
  • Energy and fertilizer markets. Natural gas and shale dynamics, energy policy uncertainty, and fertilizer price spikes that ripple through to farm input costs.
  • Food, consumers, and sustainability. Food price inflation, consumer willingness to pay for food safety and new technologies, and how the dairy sector is approaching net-zero goals.

Extension and Service

Extension is a core part of my appointment. I deliver input-cost and market outlook briefings at the annual Idaho Ag Outlook, teach multi-hour grain risk management workshops for producers across northern, southern, and eastern Idaho, and present regularly at the WSU Wheat Academy and regional cereals conferences. I also write peer-reviewed extension bulletins on grain marketing, hedging, and crop insurance, and contribute regular columns on fertilizer and fuel markets to AgProud Idaho, including the outlet's most-downloaded article of 2024.

Within the profession, I serve on the Editorial Council of the Journal of Agricultural and Applied Economics, as an Associate Editor of the Australian Journal of Agricultural and Resource Economics, and as a member of the National Wheat Improvement Committee.

Students

I have chaired or co-chaired sixteen graduate committees. Recent graduates have continued to Ph.D. programs at Texas A&M, Texas Tech, Washington State, and the University of Illinois, and to positions at Austin Energy, Decision Innovation Solutions, and State Farm. I currently advise Aavash Shrestha (M.S., consumer valuation of low-carbon dairy products) and two postdoctoral researchers, Jung-Keon Jo and Qi Zhang.

Prospective students. I welcome inquiries from students interested in commodity and energy markets, risk management, and applied econometrics.
